The first simulation is just Langton’s ant. So you can see a bunch of lil dudes shmoovin around. Though because there are multiple, the way they interact is pretty interesting because they can overwrite what each other wrote. This feels a bit analogous to a race condition (well, it kinda is, but it feels like a very good visual example of that I guess). There are also occasionally times when the ants will undo the trails made by the other.

The second simulation starts out kind of fast, but I quite like how it dramatically slows down over time. Often the ants are just bumbling around and then make a little bit of progress. Though generally when they touch each other that speeds them up. Not entirely sure why this happens, but I think it is interesting.

The third simulation is the most chaotic. Similar to the second simulation, there aren't actually many ants exploring out in trails outside. They mostly bumble around at the edges. However, unlike in the second one, they move fast enough to generally connect each other into a single blob. Their efforts feel a little bit less collaborative since there isn’t quite the same “spurring each other on when they touch”, but they do certainly all collaborate in the large noisy blob with the goal of making that blob bigger.
